Town

The town of Carratraca is located 45 km down the A-357 road from Malaga, being magnificently connected to the cosmopolitan Costa del Sol and the city of Marbella; so the car trip is covered in just 35 minutes. The study of the future Guadalhorce highway, which will connect it with the Costa del Sol, is currently being processed. Carratraca has been a benchmark in health tourism for more than two centuries, although the properties of the sulfurous springs were discovered much earlier. The origin of its famous spa can be found in the Roman baths and the Arab baths, which were created in the municipality to take advantage of the mineral-medicinal powers of its spring. The town has been declared a Site of Cultural Interest. It is very interesting to visit the spa, inaugurated in 1855; the mansion of Mrs. Trinidad Grund, the bullring inaugurated in 1878 and the church of Our Lady of Health. In its surroundings you can visit the Conde de Guadalhorce reservoir and the gorge with the surprising Caminito del Rey. Enjoy nature in its purest state in the heart of Andalusia, where you can enjoy a healthier life away from the pandemic.
